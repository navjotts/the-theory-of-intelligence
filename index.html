Thoughts on Intelligence

Humans

We start with Humans, and go backwards in the 'Evolution of Intelligence'.

Human babies start with

  • pattern-matching
  • concept of 'selfishness' (survival being a subset)

They have at their disposal – sensory observations (vision, sound, touch, smell, taste).

They use the pattern-matching to develop more and more understanding of this world – but most of that learning is centered around the single concept of 'selfishness/survival'.
(example: recognizing parents, safe corner of the house, toys/sounds that soothe them etc.)

Next they use the pattern-matching to develop some basic communication paradigm (language) – this enables the planting of 'concept of a concept'.

As soon as they reach the above stage of basic language skills, parents are able to 'teach' different concepts to them.
(babies use the rich language and communication with other humans to subconsciously develop the 'concept of a concept')

Showing a baby 1 ball to teach 'ONE' is pattern-matching – but showing 2 balls to teach 'TWO' and further, starts to be in the realm of the baby starting to understand the concept of a Number System.

  

Once powered with the skill of developing a concept (at will), the baby's mind grows exponentially fast, building hierarchies of concepts and doing a 'pattern matching on concept' itself.

Hence, the capability to develop a 'rich language paradigm' to communicate/plant/understand a concept – seems to be the biggest missing piece between human-level intelligence v/s

  • animal-level intelligence
  • current Deep Learning approaches

Example:
Let's say we want to teach a 5 year old human-kid (already having the core communication skills and the 'concept of a concept') – to open the door when the door bell rings

  1. you show the door
  2. ring the bell
  3. open the door

(a concept being formed with the above underlines)
Show once the above pattern – and it should be enough for the task to be successfully done.

Why can't we do the same with a dog?
Because a dog's intelligence system does not have the capability to develop much beyond the basic selfish/survival concept it is born with.
(1 famous concept a dog develops is 'loyalty' – but that could be put under the concept of 'selfish' again)

It can do a pattern-match, but mostly using the sensory observations available – hence it can see the door opening, or hear the bell ringing, and try to relate it to someone entering – and it can bark (or react) as a result, but its extremely hard for the dog's brain to capture the concept that the door is to be opened when the door bell rings.
(if possible, it will require extensive training to teach the dog this – which is kind of similar to what happens below)

Similarly, for the current (pattern-matching based) Deep Learning approaches to learn the same Door-Bell example, it might need thousands of such videos where the door was opened when the bell rang, and may be then it can 'learn a pattern'

  • on top of that, it probably fails if someone knocks on the door, instead of ringing the bell
  • if one says that the above can be solved by having a training set where 'knocks' also opened the door, then what about someone shouting "Open the door!"?

And so on, one can keep on coming up with more unexpected variations where a simple task could fail.
(that's why Deep Learning requires a large amount of data to be able to function in a real world scenario)

Point being – in the real complex world, the permutations are too huge to be captured in a 'pattern' – hence humans needed to start learning/capturing 'concepts' to go beyond animal-level intelligence.
(1 Higher level of Abstraction)

Capturing Concepts v/s Capturing Patterns

Pattern → hierarchical (smaller patterns can make bigger patterns)

Concept →

  • abstraction of patterns (given 'x' patterns, humans are capable of capturning a concept from them)
  • hierarchical (smaller concepts can make bigger concepts)

Patterns can be captured/learnt by the sensory inputs provided to us at birth.
Concepts being 1 higher level of abstraction, need a separate medium to capture/learn them – Language.

Animals (mostly) are capable of doing pattern-matching on sensory observations.
Humans are capable of doing a pattern-matching on concepts.

In the current Deep Learning approach, we could have the same number of neurons as a human brain – but it uses that only to do a pattern-matching of observations.
It needs the Layer of Concepts – but for that it needs the big missing piece - Language.

Animals

We go 1 step backwards now, in the Evolution of Intelligence.

As discussed, animals are (mostly) capable of doing pattern-matching on sensory observations.

They start with

  • pattern-matching
  • same concept of 'selfishness' (as humans)

What they don't have is enough neurons to develop any sort of 'rich' communication paradigm (Language).
That limits their intelligence to never touch the layer of concepts.
Now, some animals do show higher degrees of intelligence, and are capable to capture more concepts than the pure selfishness/survival – but given the lack of a rich language, they are very limited in the complexity of concepts they can learn/capture.

Micro-organisms

We go another step backwards now, in the Evolution of Intelligence – to let's say the 1st intellient living organisms on earth.

These also start with 1 'core' concept – the concept of 'selish survival'.
Whether they have any pattern-matching or not, depends on which spectrum of the evolution of life on earth we are discussing, but

  • either they have some basic pattern-matching
  • or they use 'randomness' of atomic movement to fulfill their core concept of 'selish survival'
